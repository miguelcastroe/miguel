PART I/WORK

SELECTED
WORK

Ideas that turned overlooked tensions into real-world value.

MOUTHPAD^
★ Cannes Lions Grand Prix
Disability was not the limitation. The interface was. →
Hidden friction

Most interfaces still assume a narrow idea of the body: hands, screens, gestures, and devices designed for a default user.

Idea

Reframe the tongue as a discreet, powerful human interface.

Human value

MouthPad^ turns accessibility into interface evolution. It expands what technology can recognize as human input.

VANISHING EMAILS
★ Cannes Lions Innovation
Digital waste was hiding in the habit of keeping everything. →
Hidden friction

People do not experience email storage as pollution. Digital waste feels invisible because it has no physical form.

Idea

Turn email deletion into a sustainability action.

Human value

The project made absence useful. Less storage became less waste, and deleting became a small but tangible environmental behavior.

BEHIND THE FEAR
★ Cannes Lions Shortlist
Violence was hidden inside communication people thought they understood. →
Hidden friction

Abuse often hides in plain sight, behind ordinary messages, patterns, and digital traces people overlook.

Idea

Use the invisible structure of email to make hidden violence discoverable.

Human value

People did not just receive a message about hidden violence. They had to uncover it themselves. Discovery was the emotional mechanism.

EN ONE
★ Latin American EFFIE
For micro-merchants, payment speed is not convenience. It is trust. →
Hidden friction

Delayed payments create uncertainty for people whose business depends on daily cash flow.

Idea

Turn speed into a cultural promise.

Human value

Fast payment became more than a feature; it became a reason to trust. Immediacy was made familiar, local, and credible.

PART II/THINKING

HOW THE WORK
GETS MADE REAL.

The work above is the proof.

What follows is the operating logic behind it: the role I am building toward, and the full journey I carry to move an idea from hidden friction to reality.

CREATIVE AGENCIES AND NETWORKS ARE NOT SHORT ON TALENT OR IDEAS.

The gap is ownership of the full creative journey.

Too many ideas disappear not because they are weak, but because the journey gets fragmented: someone thinks, someone sells, someone produces, someone protects, someone pushes.

But the best ideas need continuity.

A role built for continuity

This is the function I am building toward: not another layer, not a handoff role, not a connector standing between people.

A creative function that creates, sharpens, argues, structures, and executes as one continuous chain.

The full journey below is how that role behaves in practice.

Continue to the full journey ↓

THE FULL
JOURNEY

From friction to idea, from idea to case, from case to path, from path to execution.

Not as separate tasks. As one continuous creative responsibility.

01

FIND THE FRICTION

I look for the thing people feel but have stopped questioning.

Before there is an idea, there is usually a tension hiding inside the situation.

A behavior that feels normal. A system people no longer notice. A contradiction the brief has not named. A problem people feel but cannot articulate.

That is where the work begins.

↓
02

CREATE THE IDEA

I turn that friction into a clear creative proposition.

The idea is not a decoration added after the strategy.

It is the sharpest possible way to make the friction visible, useful, emotional, or impossible to ignore.

A strong idea should be simple enough to repeat and strong enough to change how people see the problem.

↓
03

BUILD THE CASE

I give the idea the language, logic, and evidence it needs to be believed.

Ideas rarely move on potential alone.

They need a case: why this matters, why now, why this brand has the right to do it, why people should care, and why the idea can survive reality.

The case is not a presentation layer. It is part of making the idea real.

↓
04

SHAPE THE PATH

I define how the idea can move through reality.

Every idea faces constraints: time, budget, approvals, production, politics, technology, timing, fear, and doubt.

The work is not to ignore those constraints. The work is to use them to shape the path.

↓
05

MAKE IT LAND

I stay with the idea until it becomes real.

The hardest part is rarely having the idea.

It is keeping the idea alive through decisions, pressure, ambiguity, and execution.

Making it land means carrying the same intention from the first tension to the final output.
